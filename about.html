Wood Types

Always 100% Reclaimed Wood

KOA

Koa is part of the Acacia family of trees and the unique color and beautiful wood grain of Hawaiian Koa is recognized as one of the finest textured woods in the world. Koa wood can grow up to 100 feet and takes 50 to 70 years to mature. In ancient Hawaii, Koa wood was harvested exclusively for the alii class or royalty. The huge logs were crafted into sculpted images of gods.


MONKEYPOD

Named after the spiral-shaped fruit pods that the tree bears, the monkeypod tree tends to grow in Central and South America, but can also thrive in other tropical regions of the world. The color of monkeypod wood tends to be a golden/dark brown with a moderate to natural luster.


LYCHEE

This lumber is derived from the subtropical evergreen tree that produces lychee fruits which are edible. The wood is incredibly dense allowing it to take an incredible natural polish. Figured or color patterns are common adding to the beauty of the wood. Originally from China, many areas of the world have propogated the tree for its distinct fruit.
